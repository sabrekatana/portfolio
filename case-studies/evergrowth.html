Overview
Transitioning careers can be challenging, especially if you don't know how to translate your past experience into skills that can land you the role you want. With so many services out there, it can be even harder to decide which ones will serve you best. Finding information to start you on your new path should be easy to navigate, and should leave you feeling empowered.

The Challenge
Evergrowth Coach LLC is a career development service that aims to help those without traditional work experience transition into a new career. As the owner and operator, Jennifer does not want her company to come across as a "resume mill", and therefore wanted to make sure that users knew they would be getting a personalized experience and that they would leave feeling inspired and confident.
The Solution
Redesigning the website for Evergrowth Coach was all about identifying the users' needs and highlighting the company's strengths, as well as what set them apart from competitors. This new design makes the information clear and accessible, creating a great user experience as they navigate finding a service that works best for them.
Summary
After research and an interview with the owner of Evergrowth Coach LLC, our team of four completed a website redesign. Our goal was to improve the website to better represent Jennifer and her company's mission as well as improve the user's experience to allow them to benefit from the provided services.

Research
Interview with the client
As we began this project, it was first important to gain an understanding of the function of the client’s current site, what she felt was or wasn’t working, and what her clients were experiencing as pain points. We sought to understand which design elements were most important to Jennifer, what she needed to get across to clients that she wasn’t already, and what kind of goals she had for her company. We found these answers through an assessment of her original site as well as an interview with Jennifer. Our interview questions included: 

Guiding questions:
  • Based on any user feedback you’ve received related to the website, what do current site users like/dislike about the website? What have they/are they asking for?
  • What are 2-3 things you want visitors to your website to know from the home page?
  • Do you have any other clients you’d like to target besides service members and women in the workforce?
  • What are the strengths of your company?
Pain Points
Through examining the website based on the perspective of a client, we were able to assess several specific pain points that users would encounter. These pain points included:

  • Being sent a link to the site but not knowing where to locate information discussed
  • Information overload
  • Some confusing buttons & symbols (RSVPs buttons, package buttons, awkward arrows)
  • Dead end on the newsletter - subscribe tab (what if user changed mind)
  • Excessive scrolling with the home page being the only area to find services offered
  • Some color contrast insufficiency makes it hard to see text (ex. Light brown regular sized text on white background)
  • No separate contact area in case the user has questions
Competetive Analysis
We examined three similar career coaching websites to analyze how their designs funneled users to their services. Our team identified the strengths and overall experience of navigating The Muse, Top Stack Resume, and Resumes Planet. 
By assessing these websites from the perspective of a user, we created a list of important improvements we should implement in Evergrowth Coach’s website. 

  • Make visuals and typography more accessible
  • Minimize irrelevant content on homepage so that services offered are easier to find
  • Organize other resources on the website to their respective pages
  • Produce a consistent color palette that aligns with the Evergrowth Coach brand
Ideation
Personas
The client had provided us with her intended target audience for her services. The career coaching services on the website were catered to women and veteran job-seekers looking to progress in their careers. Based on the information that Jennifer provided to us on her target clients, we developed two personas to better understand the problems and goals that represent the main users of the website.
Persona: Former service member
This client might be having a hard time translating their experience in a way that recruiters would have an easy time understanding and find applicable to their positions. They like to work with others who have been in the service and understand the transition they are going through.

User Stories
Through our primary research, we were able to determine not only what the clients goals were with her site, but also what users goals were as they visited. We determined some of the most important things for a user to be able to do were:

  • See Evergrowth's client services at a glance, with all relevant information to be able to compare options
  • Find themselves in the clientele she aims to help
  • Access other relevant resources such as the newsletter sign up, free resources, and media in lieu of purchasing a package
  • Gain an understanding of who Jennifer is and why she is the best person to help them with their resume and career coaching
  • Have an easy navigation when contacting Jennifer
Site Map
Upon evaluating the state of navigation of the client's original site, we determined that the user was having to take too many steps to find the most relevant information. The sorting of information was also hard to understand and navigate. As we redesigned the site map, it was important for us to consolidate information, put the most important information to the client and her users on the homepage, and make clear the content of the site with the different categories.
User Flows
Utilizing the sitemap, we were able to then arrange the users’ routes to visually understand how the user can navigate to their goals in the most simplified manner. Therefore, creating the user flow allowed us to understand the journey of the user and following a few revisions, we drafted the main routes that would guide our redesign.
Design
Sketches
After generating some ideas with the team, we came up with sketches to begin outlining how to organize and lay out the clients web flows. We sketched out our ideas and were able to have a better idea of how the website would look and were able to move forward to the wireframes and high fidelity screens. These sketches were a way to bring our user flows to life and were the start of beginning an intuitive user experience.
Low Fidelity Wireframes
We turned the sketches into low fidelity wireframes. Creating these wireframes allowed us to better understand the spacing and elemental designs of each screen. We prioritized the user’s navigation experience by making sure the next step in the user flow was clear.
The user could navigate back to the previous screen or home section by adding a back button when the user was finished paying for their package or when they filled out a form. Additionally, we accounted for easier social media access by condensing down the podcast section into one area and organizing the novels and creating an additional page for all articles.
Style Guide
To set a guideline for our design and establish consistency we created a style guide that incorporated our choice of font, colors, grid, imagery and logo. Our client’s color inspiration stemmed from the idea of growth; therefore, our team enhanced this idea by combining colors that evoked feelings of growth while also accounting for the sense of confidence the user experiences while navigating the content of the site.
High Fidelity Wireframes
Our team collaborated on 20 high fidelity wireframes for four main sectors: landing page, about section, services section and media section. We valued an easy to navigate experience that established a sense of confidence for the user both in using the site and in the content of the site. This process was highly iterative, and after many iterations we were able to reach our final UI. A final walkthrough occurred to fine tune the details of each page.
When a user first enters the company’s website, they will land on the homepage. Prior to our team’s redesign, information overload on the homepage made it difficult to funnel users directly to service packages. Our team removed unnecessary content from the homepage and organized it to clearly emphasize the brand attributes of Evergrowth Coach and its services.
Hand-off
Client Presentation
During our final presentation, we demonstrated the navigation process by reviewing each step the user could take as well as how we addressed high priority issues throughout the design. We also utilized this time to receive any client feedback related to the re-design. The client expressed a need for additional information surrounding color; therefore, our team reviewed how we were able to adhere to industry standards while also incorporating the original inspiration of the client’s color choice. Overall, the presentation went well and the client was happy to see her work being displayed in a user-friendly way. We shared our drive that contained the style guide, final UI, logo, and updated marketing flyer to match the redesign.
Key Takeaways
This was not only my first client project, but also my first project working in a team. At first I was nervous to see how a group of people would be able to work on one design, but I was surprised at how easy it was. Our group established good communication from the start, meeting over video chat as often as possible to make sure everyone was getting the same information as we made decisions. Once we established our design elements, we were all able to actively work on the same design file and have a cohesive end product.
This project gave me a new perspective on collaborating, as we not only had to work as a team, we also had to keep the client's ideas, motivations, goals, and insights at the forefront of all of our decision making.
